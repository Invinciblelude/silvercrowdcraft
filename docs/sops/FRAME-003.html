FRAME-003: Wall Framing

Trade Framing
Duration 60 hours
Crew Size 4 people

üìã Prerequisites

  • Floor deck complete

üß∞ Materials Needed

  • 2x4 or 2x6 studs (per plan, typically 16 inch OC)
  • 2x4 or 2x6 top and bottom plates (3 plates total: bottom, top, double top)
  • Headers (sized per span table for openings)
  • Jack studs and king studs for openings
  • Cripple studs above/below openings
  • 16d nails or framing screws
  • Hurricane ties/straps for top plate connection

üîß Tools Required

  • Circular saw
  • Framing nailer
  • Hammer
  • Level (4ft and 8ft)
  • Speed square
  • Chalk line
  • Measuring tape
Hard hat
Safety glasses
Tool belt
Fall protection if >6ft
Steel-toe boots
Gloves

üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Snap chalk lines on deck for wall locations per architectural plan
  2. Cut bottom plate and top plates to length for each wall
  3. Mark stud locations on plates (16 inch OC typical)
  4. Mark door and window openings on plates
  5. Cut studs to length (typically 92-5/8 inches for 8ft ceiling)
  6. Assemble wall flat on deck: nail studs between bottom and top plates
  7. Install headers, jack studs, king studs, and cripples at openings
  8. Raise wall and brace plumb
  9. Nail bottom plate to deck/sill plate
  10. Nail double top plate (lapping corners for tie-in)
  11. Install blocking/fire blocking at mid-height if required by code
  12. Repeat for all walls
  13. Connect walls at corners with 3-stud corners or equivalent
  14. Install hurricane ties or straps at top plate/rafter connection

‚úÖ Quality Control Checks

All walls are plumb (check every 10 feet)
Stud spacing is consistent (16 inch OC)
Headers are sized correctly for span
Corners are square and nailed properly
Double top plate laps at corners (minimum 4ft lap)
Opening dimensions match plan

  • Framing inspection before sheathing/insulation
