Step-by-Step Framing Instructions:

1
Layout Foundation Lines

On your foundation slab, snap chalk lines for:

  • Exterior walls: 25' wide x 24' deep rectangle (verify exact dims from Sheet 07)
  • Interior wall 1: Separates bedroom from closet (runs east-west)
  • Interior wall 2: Separates bedroom from bathroom (runs north-south)
  • Closet/Bath divider: Small wall between closet and bath
2
Frame Exterior Walls

Build each exterior wall flat on the slab:

  • North wall: 25' long, 8' tall, 2x6 studs @ 16" O.C.
  • East wall: 24' long, 8' tall, 2x6 studs @ 16" O.C.
  • South wall: 25' long, has DOOR opening (leave 3' gap), 2x6 studs
  • West wall: 24' long (this ties into main house - see Section 8)
  • Add headers over windows (north wall has 2 windows)
  • Tip up walls and brace temporarily
3
Frame Interior Walls

Interior walls are 2x4 (non-load-bearing):

  • Bedroom/Closet wall: Runs east-west, ‚âà15' long
  • Bedroom/Bath wall: Runs north-south, ‚âà14' long, has door opening
  • Closet/Bath divider: Short wall, ‚âà8' long
  • Leave door openings as shown on plans
4
Install Top Plates & Prepare for Roof
  • Install double top plates on ALL walls
  • Top plates tie walls together and support roof
  • Make sure plates overlap at corners
  • Check Sheet 15 for roof framing that goes above this section

üö® CRITICAL FIRST STEP

You MUST establish this connection point first! It defines where the new addition starts and how it ties into existing structure. Get this wrong and everything else will be off.

What You're Connecting To:

  • Existing house: 2,611 SF (already standing)
  • New addition: 3,200 SF (what you're framing)
  • Connection wall: Where they meet - this is the western edge of your addition
  • Sheet 07 shows the dotted line indicating "existing" vs "new"

Steps:

1
Verify Existing House Measurements
  • Measure the existing wall where addition will attach
  • Compare to Sheet 07 - make sure dimensions match reality
  • Mark exactly where new addition starts
  • This establishes your "datum" - starting point for all measurements
2
Remove Existing Siding (If Needed)
  • Where new walls tie in, you may need to remove exterior siding from existing house
  • Exposes existing framing so you can attach new framing directly
  • Check Sheet 14 for connection details
