üèóÔ∏è Wall Framing - Step by Step

1
Mark Your Layout on the Foundation

Before you cut any wood, snap chalk lines on your foundation slab showing where every wall will go.

  • Use the floor plan - all those dimension lines (like "10'-6", "12'-0") tell you exactly where walls go
  • Measure from corners - start at one corner and measure along to mark each wall location
  • Mark both sides - walls have thickness (usually 3.5" for interior, 5.5" for exterior)
  • Double-check squares - use 3-4-5 triangle method to ensure corners are 90¬∞
2
Frame Exterior Walls First

The outside perimeter walls give you structure to work from.

  • Wall Height: Your plans show 8'-0" (8 feet) walls for first floor
  • Stud Spacing: 16" on center (that means measure 16" from center of one stud to center of next)
  • Materials:
    • Bottom plate: 2x6 (exterior walls)
    • Top plate: Double 2x6 (two stacked on top)
    • Studs: 2x6 vertical boards every 16"
  • Window/Door Openings: Your plans show openings - you need headers (horizontal beams) above these
üí° Reading Wall Dimensions:

When you see "10'-6" on the plan, that means 10 feet and 6 inches. The small dash after the number is feet, and the quote after is inches.

3
Frame Interior Load-Bearing Walls

These walls support the roof and any second floor weight.

  • How to identify: Look at the structural plan - walls marked with special symbols (darker lines, circled numbers)
  • Main load-bearing walls run:
    • Down the center of the house (north-south)
    • Where the roof changes direction
    • Under any beams shown on the roof plan
  • Materials: Same as exterior (2x6 or as shown on plans)
4
Frame Interior Non-Load-Bearing Walls

These are just room dividers - they don't support anything above them.

  • Materials: Can use 2x4 (cheaper and thinner)
  • Spacing: Still 16" on center
  • Height: Same as exterior walls (8'-0")
  • These create: Bedrooms, bathrooms, closets, hallways

Window & Door Headers

Every window and door needs a header - a horizontal beam that carries the load above the opening.

  • Small openings (up to 3 feet): Use 2x6 or 2x8
  • Medium openings (3-6 feet): Use 2x10 or 2x12
  • Large openings (6+ feet): Check structural details - might need engineered beam
  • Your plans show exact header sizes - look for notes like "2x8 HDR" or "2x10 HEADER"

Roof Framing Steps:

1
Install Ridge Beams First

The ridge beam is the very top of the roof - it runs along the peak.

  • Your plans show: Multiple ridge beams (house has different roof sections)
  • Look for labels like: "TYP @ GLUE END" or "TYP @ RIDGE"
  • Size: Plans will say something like "2x10" or "LVL BEAM"
  • Height: Check the elevation views (side views of house) for ridge height
2
Install Hip & Valley Beams

Where different roof sections meet, you need hip and valley beams.

  • Hip: Outside corner where two roof slopes meet (goes from ridge to wall)
  • Valley: Inside corner where two roof slopes meet (collects water)
  • On your plan: These show as diagonal red/orange lines
  • Size: Usually 2x8 or 2x10 (check plans)
3
Install Rafters

Rafters are the sloped boards that create the roof surface.

  • Spacing: Your plans show "24" O.C." - that means 24 inches on center
  • Size: Look for notes like "2x8 RAFTER" or "2x10 RAFTER"
  • Layout: They run from the ridge beam down to the top of the wall
  • Cuts:
    • Bird's mouth: Notch where rafter sits on top plate
    • Plumb cut: Angled cut at top that meets ridge
    • Tail cut: Decorative cut at bottom (overhang)
üí° Reading Roof Slopes:

Your plans show roof pitch like "4:12" - this means: for every 12 inches horizontal, the roof rises 4 inches. This determines how steep your rafters are.

Beam Schedule (From Your Plans):

Your plans have a "BEAM SCHEDULE" table (upper right corner of roof plan). Here's what it means:

Mark Size & Type What It Means
B1 3.5"x11.25" LVL Laminated Veneer Lumber - main support beam
B2 5.25"x11.25" LVL Thicker LVL - for wider spans
B3, B4, etc. Various sizes Each "B" with number corresponds to a location on the plan

Find the "B1" label on your plan, then look at the schedule to see what beam to use there!

üîß Shear Walls - Extra Strong Walls for Safety

Shear Wall Plan - The thick lines show which walls need special reinforcement

What is a Shear Wall?

Shear walls resist sideways forces like:

  • Earthquakes - lateral movement
  • High winds - pushing on the house
  • Prevents racking - house twisting or leaning

How to Build Shear Walls:

1
Identify Shear Walls on Your Plan

Look for walls marked with special symbols:

  • Thick lines on the shear wall plan
  • Circled numbers like ‚ë†, ‚ë°, ‚ë¢
  • Notes like "16d @ 4" O.C." - this tells you nailing pattern
  • Your house has shear walls: Around perimeter and at key interior locations
2
Frame Wall Normally, But...

Shear walls are framed like regular walls, with these additions:

  • Sheathing: Must use plywood or OSB (not just drywall)
  • Thickness: Plans will specify - usually 15/32" or 1/2" plywood
  • Special nailing: More nails, closer together than normal walls
  • Anchor bolts: Bottom plate must be bolted to foundation (not just nailed)
3
Follow the Nailing Schedule

Your plans show exact nailing patterns - this is CRITICAL for shear walls!

  • "16d @ 4" O.C." means:
    • Use 16-penny (16d) nails
    • Space them 4 inches apart
    • O.C. = on center (center to center)
  • Edge nailing: Closer spacing (2", 3", or 4") around perimeter of plywood
  • Field nailing: Wider spacing (usually 12") in the middle of plywood
  • DO NOT SKIP NAILS - inspector will check this!
‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT:

Shear walls MUST be built exactly as shown on the structural plans. Building inspectors will specifically check these. If done wrong, you'll have to tear them out and redo them!

üìÖ Step-by-Step Build Sequence

Follow this exact order - each step depends on the previous one being complete.

1
Foundation Complete & Clean
  • Foundation slab poured and cured (7 days minimum)
  • Anchor bolts installed (should be sticking up from concrete)
  • Sweep slab clean of dirt and debris
  • Check for level - slab should be within 1/4" over 10 feet
2
Layout All Walls
  • Snap chalk lines for ALL walls (exterior and interior)
  • Mark door and window openings
  • Double-check all dimensions against floor plan
  • Verify corners are square (3-4-5 method)
  • Time estimate: 1-2 days
3
Frame Exterior Walls
  • Build walls flat on slab, then tip up into place
  • Install bottom plate first (drill for anchor bolts)
  • Add studs and top plates
  • Create rough openings for doors and windows
  • Install headers above openings
  • Brace walls temporarily until connected
  • Time estimate: 3-5 days
4
Frame Interior Load-Bearing Walls
  • Frame walls that support roof/beams (per structural plan)
  • These tie exterior walls together
  • Install any steel columns or posts shown on plans
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
5
Install Shear Wall Plywood & Hold-Downs
  • Apply plywood to shear walls (per shear wall plan)
  • Follow exact nailing schedule
  • Install hold-down brackets
  • CALL FOR INSPECTION before proceeding
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
6
Frame Interior Non-Load-Bearing Walls
  • Frame bedroom walls, bathroom walls, closets
  • These can be 2x4 instead of 2x6
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
7
Install Main Support Beams
  • Install LVL beams per beam schedule
  • These sit on top of load-bearing walls
  • Support roof structure
  • Need help lifting - these are HEAVY
  • Time estimate: 1-2 days
8
Frame Roof - Ridge Beams
  • Install ridge beams at peak of roof
  • Need scaffolding or lifts
  • Brace securely
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
9
Frame Roof - Rafters
  • Install rafters 24" on center
  • Cut bird's mouth notches
  • Attach to ridge beam and top plate
  • Install hurricane ties at each rafter
  • Time estimate: 4-6 days
10
Frame Roof - Hips & Valleys
  • Install hip rafters (outside corners)
  • Install valley rafters (inside corners)
  • Install jack rafters between hip/valley and ridge
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
11
Install Roof Sheathing
  • Apply OSB or plywood to roof rafters
  • Start at bottom, work up to ridge
  • Stagger seams
  • Leave 1/8" gap between sheets for expansion
  • CALL FOR FRAMING INSPECTION
  • Time estimate: 2-3 days
