üß± Exterior Wall - Cross Section
2x6 construction, 8'-0" height

Exterior Wall Construction:

Bottom Plate: Sits on foundation, bolted every 6 feet

Studs: 2x6 boards spaced 16" apart (center to center)

Top Plates: Double 2x6 boards - ties everything together

Sheathing: OSB or plywood on exterior side (weather barrier)

Insulation: R-19 fiberglass batts between studs

Drywall: Interior finish, 1/2" thick

Interior Wall Features:

2x4 studs: Lighter and cheaper than exterior 2x6

Drywall both sides: Room divider finished on each room

What Are Shear Walls?

Purpose: Resist earthquake and wind forces (sideways movement)

How to identify: Thick lines on this plan show which walls are shear walls

Special requirements: Plywood sheathing + special nailing pattern + hold-down brackets

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: These must be built exactly as shown - inspectors will check!

How shear walls are reinforced

Key Differences:

Regular wall: Just studs with drywall

Shear wall: Studs + plywood + special nailing + hold-down brackets

Nailing: Nails every 4" around edges (vs. 12" for regular walls)
