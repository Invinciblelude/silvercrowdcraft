# Wall Framing Knowledge Base
## Complete Understanding for Accurate Blueprint-to-Illustration Conversion

## üéØ CORE PRINCIPLE
**A wall is a structural assembly that transfers roof/floor loads downward to the foundation.**

Every component has a purpose. Understanding WHY tells you HOW to build it correctly.

---

## üìê PHASE 1: FOUNDATION ATTACHMENT

### Bottom Plate (Sill Plate)
- **Purpose:** Creates a horizontal base that anchors the wall to the foundation
- **Material:** 2x6 (exterior) or 2x4 (interior)
- **Function:** 
  - Distributes wall load evenly across foundation
  - Provides platform for vertical studs
  - Must be pressure-treated if in contact with concrete
- **Connection:** Bolted to foundation with anchor bolts
- **Anchor Bolt Specs (from 728 Cordant):**
  - Size: 5/8" diameter
  - Spacing: Maximum 6'-0" on center
  - Embedment: 7" minimum into concrete
  - How to drill: 11/16" hole through plate (slightly larger than bolt)

**KEY UNDERSTANDING:** Bottom plate is the FIRST thing that happens. Foundation ‚Üí Plate ‚Üí Studs.

---

## üèóÔ∏è PHASE 2: VERTICAL STRUCTURE

### Wall Studs
- **Purpose:** Carry vertical loads from roof/floor down to foundation
- **Material:** 2x6 (exterior) or 2x4 (interior)
- **Actual Size:** 
  - "2x6" nominal = 1.5" x 5.5" actual
  - "2x4" nominal = 1.5" x 3.5" actual
- **Spacing:** 16" on center (O.C.)
  - What "16" O.C." means:
    - Measure from CENTER of one stud to CENTER of next stud
    - NOT from edge to edge!
    - 16" spacing allows drywall/sheet goods to land on studs
- **Height:** Standard 8'-0" (96 inches)

**KEY UNDERSTANDING:** Studs are the "bones" - they create the structural framework.

### Stud Layout Mathematics
For a 10'-0" long wall:
- Divide 120" by 16" = 7.5 spacings
- Need 8 studs minimum (one at each end + 6 interior)
- Typical layout: Start 16" from corner, then every 16" thereafter

**Common Stud Count Formula:**
- Short walls (< 10'): Corner studs + at 16" intervals
- Long walls: Exact count = (Length in inches √∑ 16) + 1 (for corner)

## üîó PHASE 3: HORIZONTAL CONNECTIONS

### Top Plates (Double Plate)
- **Purpose:** 
  - Ties multiple walls together
  - Provides load distribution across wall
  - Prevents wall buckling
- **Material:** Double 2x6 (exterior) or 2x4 (interior)
- **Installation:** Two boards STACKED on top of studs
- **Why Double:**
  - Bottom plate overlaps top of studs ‚Üí provides transfer surface
  - Top plate extends beyond corners ‚Üí ties adjacent walls together
  - Continuity across door/window openings

**KEY UNDERSTANDING:** Top plate creates horizontal continuity - walls become a unified system.

---

## ü™ü PHASE 4: OPENINGS (Doors & Windows)

### Headers
- **Purpose:** Carry the load ABOVE an opening (what studs can't do because they're interrupted)
- **Location:** Horizontal beam at top of opening
- **Sizing Rule of Thumb:**
  - Small openings (‚â§ 3'): 2x6 or 2x8 header
  - Medium (3'-6'): 2x10 or 2x12 header
  - Large (6'+): Consult structural engineer or beam schedule
  - Your blueprints will specify exact sizes

**How Headers Work:**
```
Weight of roof/floor above ‚Üí Transfers to header ‚Üí Header transfers to King Studs ‚Üí Down to foundation
```

### King Studs
- **Purpose:** Full-height studs on EACH SIDE of an opening
- **Function:** Carries the load that header transfers
- **Material:** Same as wall studs (2x6 or 2x4)
- **Installation:** Goes from bottom plate to top plate, adjacent to opening

### Jack Studs (Trimmer Studs)
- **Purpose:** Supports the header directly
- **Material:** Same as wall studs
- **Height:** Shorter - from bottom plate to bottom of header
- **Installation:** Between king stud and header, typically 1-2 jacks per side
- **Why Needed:** Header sits ON the jack studs (gravity load transfer)

### Cripple Studs
- **Purpose:** Fills in above and below openings
- **Location:** 
  - Above header (between header and top plate)
  - Below sill plate (between sill and bottom plate) - less common
- **Function:** Maintains stud spacing, provides nailing for drywall
- **Installation:** Space at 16" O.C. like regular studs

### Rough Opening (R.O.) Dimensions
- **Total Opening** = Actual window/door size + shrinkage
- **Typical Shrinkage:**
  - Window: Add 1" width, 1/2" height
  - Door: Add 2" width, 2-1/2" height (for threshold)
- **Example:** 3'-0" x 4'-0" window
  - Rough opening = 37" wide x 48.5" tall
  - Header spans 37" + king stud widths

**KEY UNDERSTANDING:** Every opening weakens the wall. Headers + king/jack studs redistribute the load around the opening.

## üõ°Ô∏è PHASE 5: SHEATHING & PROTECTION

### Exterior Sheathing (OSB/Plywood)
- **Purpose:** 
  - Provides lateral strength (resists wind/earthquake forces)
  - Weather barrier base
  - Creates continuous structural diaphragm
- **Material (728 Cordant spec):** 15/32" OSB
- **Installation:** 
  - Horizontal layout (long side perpendicular to studs)
  - Stagger vertical seams
  - Gap 1/8" between sheets for expansion
  - Nails: 6d @ 6" edges, 12" field (typical)
- **Coverage:** Entire exterior wall surface

**KEY UNDERSTANDING:** Sheathing turns individual studs into a unified structural wall system.

### Insulation
- **Material:** R-19 fiberglass batts (728 Cordant spec)
- **Purpose:** Thermal barrier
- **Installation:** Fits between studs
- **Note:** NOT structural, but required by code

### Interior Drywall
- **Material:** 1/2" standard gypsum board
- **Installation:** Vertical or horizontal, nails @ 16" OC
- **Coverage:** Entire interior wall surface

## üî® CONSTRUCTION SEQUENCE (THE ORDER MATTERS!)

### Step-by-Step Wall Assembly:

**STEP 1: Layout**
- Snap chalk lines on foundation showing wall locations
- Mark anchor bolt positions
- Mark all openings (windows/doors)

**STEP 2: Bottom Plate**
- Cut plate to length
- Drill holes for anchor bolts (11/16" holes)
- Install on foundation, tight against chalk line
- Square corners using 3-4-5 triangle

**STEP 3: Cut Studs**
- All studs cut to same length (8'-0" minus plates = 96" - 5.5" = 90.5" for 2x6)
- Mark layout on plate @ 16" intervals

**STEP 4: Assemble Wall Flat**
- Lay bottom plate on slab/ground
- Position studs on 16" centers
- Install double top plate
- Square wall using diagonal measurements
- Brace temporarily

**STEP 5: Tip Wall Up**
- Lift entire assembly into place
- Align over bottom plate on foundation
- Attach to foundation with anchor bolts
- Level and plumb wall
- Brace to adjacent walls or ground

**STEP 6: Openings**
- Cut studs for rough openings
- Install headers, king studs, jack studs
- Install cripples
- Verify rough opening dimensions

**STEP 7: Sheathing**
- Apply OSB to exterior
- Nail per schedule
- Install house wrap (WRB)

**STEP 8: Insulation**
- Install batts between studs
- Full thickness coverage

**STEP 9: Drywall**
- Hang drywall
- Tape and finish

## üìä READING BLUEPRINT SPECIFICATIONS

### What Each Symbol/Note Means:

**Wall Lines:**
- Thick solid line = Exterior wall (bearing)
- Thin solid line = Interior wall (non-bearing)
- Double line = Wall thickness shown

**Dimensions:**
- 10'-6" = 10 feet, 6 inches
- @ 16" O.C. = on center, measured from center to center
- R.O. = Rough opening

**Annotations:**
- "TYP" = Typical (applies to all locations unless noted otherwise)
- "SEE SCHEDULE" = Details in separate table
- Cntr (Center) = From centerline, not edge

### Example Reading a Wall Detail:
```
EXTERIOR WALL: 2x6 @ 16" O.C., 15/32" OSB, R-19, 5/8" Anchor @ 6'-0" Max
```

**Translation:**
- Use 2x6 studs
- Space them 16" from center to center
- Sheath with 15/32" thick OSB
- Install R-19 insulation
- Bolt with 5/8" anchors, max 6 feet apart

## ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake #1: Wrong Stud Spacing
**Error:** Measuring from edge to edge instead of center to center
**Result:** Studs don't align with sheet goods, drywall doesn't land on studs
**Fix:** Always measure from CENTER of one stud to CENTER of next

### Mistake #2: Missing Jack Studs
**Error:** Header sitting on king stud alone
**Result:** Insufficient support, header sags
**Fix:** Always install jack studs to support header ends

### Mistake #3: Wrong Rough Opening
**Error:** Opening too small for window/door
**Result:** Window/door won't fit, have to reframe
**Fix:** Always add shrinkage (typically 1-2" wider than unit)

### Mistake #4: Single Top Plate
**Error:** Using only one top plate board
**Result:** Adjacent walls don't tie together properly
**Fix:** Always use DOUBLE top plates

### Mistake #5: Not Squaring Walls
**Error:** Assuming corners are square
**Result:** Windows don't open, doors bind, aesthetic issues
**Fix:** Use 3-4-5 triangle method to verify square

## üé® VISUALIZATION PRINCIPLES

### What Makes an Accurate Construction Illustration:

**1. Proportion:**
- Relative sizes must match: studs look thicker than drywall
- Spacing visually correct (stud gaps approximately equal)
- Heights realistic

**2. Layering:**
- Foundation at bottom (dark, heavy)
- Plates shown clearly (orange/yellow)
- Studs vertical and evenly spaced
- Sheathing wraps exterior
- Drywall on interior
- Order of assembly visible

**3. Connections:**
- Show how pieces connect (bolts, nails, overlaps)
- Demonstrate load paths (arrows showing forces)
- Indicate sequence where helpful

**4. Labels:**
- Every major component named
- Dimensions shown accurately
- Materials specified

**5. Context:**
- Show relationship to adjacent elements
- Indicate "inside" vs "outside" of house
- Reference to actual blueprint specifications
